Infrastructure vs. Obsolescence

May 20, 2026 · 10 companies scored · AI's dual effect on enterprise technology — compounding the value of essential infrastructure while erasing the value of human process intermediaries

Issue #10 draws the sharpest contrast the ADS framework has produced: the same AI buildout that threatens Navient (78) and WNS (68) is compounding the value of Arm Holdings (14) and Datadog (22). AI is not a uniform risk — it is a divergence engine. Companies that own essential infrastructure or proprietary data compound in an AI world. Companies that mediate human processes get automated around. Paycom (46) is this issue's most instructive case: its best AI innovation is simultaneously its biggest revenue headwind.

  • Arm is the lowest ADS in Issue #10 — and among the lowest in the full 100-company universe — because it is the architectural foundation that all AI chips are built on. Every AI training cluster, every inference chip, every edge AI device uses Arm architecture. The royalty model means Arm collects a tax on every AI chip shipped, at zero incremental operating cost. The more AI scales, the more royalties flow.
  • The Armv9 architecture transition carries a 2–3x royalty rate premium over Armv8. As AI workloads drive chipmakers to adopt Armv9 (enhanced matrix math, power efficiency, security), Arm's revenue per chip shipped expands without volume growth. FY2026 revenue was $4.92B (+23% YoY). FY2031 guidance: $25B. The compounding of volume growth and rate expansion makes this a unique financial model in the semiconductor industry.
  • The AGI CPU announced in March 2026 — Arm's first in-house chip in 35 years — transforms the company from IP licensor to vertically integrated AI compute provider. Meta is the lead co-development partner. OpenAI, Cloudflare, SAP, and SK Telecom committed as customers. Estimated $15B annual revenue potential in 5 years if the AGI CPU executes.
Key trigger: AGI CPU tape-out schedule and initial customer benchmarks (expected 2027). The royalty business alone is exceptional — the AGI CPU is the upside scenario. If Meta deploys AGI CPU in production AI training clusters by FY2028, the product viability is proven. If it delays, the royalty business sustains independently.
