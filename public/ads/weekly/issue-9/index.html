ADS Weekly · Issue #9

The Knowledge Economy Reckoning

May 13, 2026 · 10 companies scored · When AI reprices human expertise in healthcare, law, media, and education

Issue #9 turns the lens on the knowledge economy — the sectors where human expertise was the product. LegalZoom (72) charged $300 to fill out an LLC form. Gannett (72) charged advertising dollars to have a human write a story. 2U (70) charged a revenue share to put a professor's lecture online. All three now compete with a free API call. Meanwhile, Verisk (32) and Veeva (36) demonstrate that proprietary data moats compound in an AI world, and Tempus AI (28) is this issue's structural winner — it IS the AI doing the displacing.

  • Gannett employs over 11,000 journalists and editorial staff. The core tasks — writing game recaps, election result summaries, earnings briefs, weather updates, and police blotter reports — are among the most automatable in professional services. AI-generated local news content is already competing for the same readership from AP, Arc Publishing, and direct AI-synthesis tools.
  • Digital ad revenue fell 14% year-over-year in the most recent quarter. This is a structural decline driven by search and social platform disintermediation, now amplified by AI-generated content that eliminates the human writing step. Google AI Overviews and similar systems displace article-discovery traffic that fuels Gannett's CPM revenue model.
  • Revenue durability (D2: 90) is the most alarming dimension — the company's business model is already in terminal decline and AI accelerates the timeline rather than initiating it. Gannett's attempted pivots (platform licensing, aggressive cost-cutting) are repositioning into a segment where AI has the structural cost and speed advantage.
Key trigger: Google AI Overviews query share exceeding 40% for local news categories is the displacement threshold. At that penetration, the display advertising model underwriting Gannett's newsroom economics collapses structurally rather than cyclically.

  • LegalZoom's core product — templated legal document generation for business formation, wills, and contracts — is now replicable with a free LLM prompt. Claude, ChatGPT, and Harvey AI can draft an LLC operating agreement or simple will in seconds with no subscription required, directly threatening the $200–$500 LegalZoom charges for the same output.
  • The intermediation risk dimension (D4: 90) is the highest in this issue. LegalZoom sits exactly between the customer and the underlying legal form — AI is collapsing that intermediary position by enabling direct self-service at zero marginal cost. The company's moat was information asymmetry and form complexity; AI eliminates both.
  • The subscription pivot and attorney network are the strategic lifelines, but subscription ARR growth has stalled and the attorney matchmaking segment faces free-tier competition. The company is transitioning from a form-filing business to a platform business in a market where AI has made the original value proposition obsolete.
Key trigger: LLM accuracy on state-specific LLC and estate document generation reaching 95%+ with user verification tools represents the commoditization threshold. At that point, LegalZoom's form-filing revenue (historically ~60% of total) faces existential substitution.

  • 2U's OPM model — paying universities upfront for revenue-share rights on online degrees — requires sustained enrollment and pricing power eroding on both ends. AI tutoring platforms (Khan Academy's Khanmigo, Coursera's AI assistant) now replicate the instructional function of 2U's online programs at a fraction of the cost.
  • Revenue durability (D2: 90) reflects an already-stressed business model. The company filed for Chapter 11 in 2023 with ~$950M in debt and emerged restructured, but into a market where universities have less financial incentive to outsource and more capability to deliver online programs using AI-powered tools in-house.
  • The OPM model was predicated on the idea that online delivery was technically and operationally complex — requiring specialized expertise in content production, adaptive learning, and student support. AI has commoditized all three functions, erasing the justification for 2U's revenue-share premium.
Key trigger: University contract non-renewals accelerating above 15% annually would signal the structural end of the OPM intermediary model. The 2023 bankruptcy forced renegotiations on dozens of contracts at less favorable terms; the next renewal cycle will reveal whether the model has a viable equilibrium.

  • Teladoc's $18.5B acquisition of Livongo at peak valuation resulted in a $13.7B goodwill impairment — the most visible sign that the market has repriced the telehealth model. The company is now valued at ~$1.5B on $2.5B+ in revenue, reflecting structural skepticism about the physician-as-intermediary model.

  • Instructure's Canvas LMS dominates U.S. higher education with ~30% market share and long-term institutional contracts — meaningful revenue durability. But the LMS is infrastructure, not intelligence. It is increasingly becoming the carrier for AI tools that may eventually displace the need for a separate LMS layer entirely.

  • Adaptation Capacity (D5: 30) is the critical weakness. Instructure has been slow to build native AI features into Canvas, relying on third-party LTI integrations while competitors build AI-first platforms from scratch. The Thoma Bravo private equity overlay adds financial engineering pressure that may constrain the R&D investment needed to adapt.
Key trigger: A major R1 university system announcing a transition to an AI-native LMS or a direct contract with an AI tutoring provider that bypasses the LMS layer would be the structural inflection point for Instructure's institutional moat.

  • Veeva has exceptional switching costs: pharmaceutical companies run clinical trials, regulatory submissions, and global sales operations on Veeva Vault.   • Revenue durability (D2: 20) reflects the non-cyclical, contractually locked-in nature of pharma R&D and commercial spending.   • Verisk's ISO policy language and actuarial databases are embedded in the regulatory and contractual fabric of U.S. insurance. Insurers file rates based on ISO loss costs — a regulatory integration that is not substitutable by AI-generated alternatives in any near-term timeframe.
  • The company's data assets — decades of claims data, property records, and catastrophe modeling calibrations — are proprietary training material that would be required to replicate the accuracy of Verisk's models. AI amplifies rather than replaces this data moat: better models trained on exclusive data widen the competitive gap.
  • Adaptation capacity (D5: 50) is above average in this issue.   • Tempus is the AI in the AI displacement equation. Its multimodal clinical dataset (genomic sequences, clinical records, imaging) and the models trained on it represent the output that is displacing manual pathology review, treatment selection, and clinical trial matching — the activities it does not perform, it makes more efficient.
  • The data network effect is the primary moat: each new patient record makes the models more accurate, which attracts more clinical partnerships, which generates more data. This is a fundamentally compounding asset in an AI-first healthcare world — the opposite dynamic from knowledge intermediaries losing value as AI commoditizes their output.
  • Revenue durability risk (D2: 20) is low because precision oncology is a growing market: AI-driven treatment selection expands the addressable opportunity by enabling protocols that weren't feasible without genomic-scale data analysis. The company's highest risk is competitive — Foundation Medicine, NVIDIA BioNeMo, and Illumina's AI initiatives — not displacement by AI itself.
Key trigger to watch (competitive, not displacement): NVIDIA BioNeMo or a similar foundational genomics model achieving open-source accuracy comparable to Tempus's clinical models would compress the proprietary data premium. The Intermediation score (D4: 10) is the lowest in this issue — Tempus is a native AI infrastructure layer, not an intermediary waiting to be removed.
