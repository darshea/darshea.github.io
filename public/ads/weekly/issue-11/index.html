The Great AI Divide

June 3, 2026 · 10 companies scored · Legacy IT services face structural displacement while AI infrastructure suppliers accelerate — the same AI buildout creates opposite trajectories

Issue #11 scores the sharpest structural divergence in the ADS framework to date. Kyndryl (70) and Xerox (70) are highest-risk companies in this issue — AI operations platforms are automating the managed IT and document workflows that define their businesses. On the opposite end, Marvell Technology (18) builds the custom silicon that powers every AI cluster, with Q2 FY27 guidance of +35% YoY confirming the AI infrastructure supercycle is accelerating, not slowing. The same AI buildout creates opposite outcomes depending on which side of the stack a company sits.

  • Kyndryl holds the highest ADS in Issue #11 at 70, reflecting the structural exposure of a pure-play managed infrastructure services provider in an era where AI is automating the monitoring, management, and incident response workflows that define its business. Spun off from IBM in November 2021, Kyndryl inherited approximately 4,000 managed services contracts serving 75% of Fortune 100 companies — a portfolio that provides near-term revenue stability but embeds multi-year structural decline risk as AIOps platforms (ServiceNow ITOM, Dynatrace, PagerDuty) automate the billable labor hours per managed environment.
  • Kyndryl's 'Bridge' transformation strategy focuses on three objectives: divesting unprofitable legacy contracts, building Kyndryl Consult as a higher-value advisory business, and transitioning to AI-augmented managed services. Financial progress is visible — margins improving from deeply negative to near breakeven. The challenge is pace: converting a 83,000-person labor-intensive delivery model into AI-augmented delivery while managing client retention and renewal economics, against IBM Consulting, Infosys, and Wipro with larger AI R&D budgets.
  • FY2026 revenue of approximately $14.7B represents decline from the $19B peak at spinoff — structural reduction driven by contract expirations and client insourcing. The mainframe services segment faces secular decline as enterprises migrate workloads to cloud-native architectures. An ADS of 70 reflects a company managing the most challenging transformation in IT services: converting a legacy labor model into an AI-era managed services provider against better-capitalized competitors with superior AI platforms.
Key trigger: Kyndryl's contract renewal economics in FY2027 are the defining monitoring signal. If revenue per contract increases at renewal, the Bridge transformation is working. If revenue per contract declines 10%+, clients are renegotiating AI efficiency savings rather than paying Kyndryl to capture them. Secondary: Kyndryl Consult revenue as percentage of total — if consulting exceeds 20% by FY2028, the higher-value pivot is materializing.

  • Xerox's ADS of 70 — tied for the highest in Issue #11 — reflects the compound structural decline of a company whose primary business (physical document printing and managed print services) is being eliminated by the digitization trend that AI is accelerating. Physical document printing has declined 8–10% annually since 2020; hybrid work permanently reduced office printing volumes; AI-powered document processing eliminates the need for documents to exist in physical form at any point in enterprise workflows.
  • Xerox's transformation has followed two tracks: IT services expansion (acquiring small managed IT service providers) and Elem additive manufacturing (3D printing for industrial applications). The IT services strategy is logical but faces scale problems — this market is dominated by substantially larger, better-capitalized providers (Dell, HPE, Leidos, CDW) against whom Xerox lacks scale and AI platform differentiation. Elem represents less than 1% of revenue.
  • The financial sustainability of Xerox's transformation is the critical variable. Free cash flow has been constrained by restructuring charges and revenue decline, limiting capital available for IT services acquisitions. Xerox's $2B market capitalization reflects skepticism about its ability to replace physical product revenue with services revenue — a transition requiring both customer relationship development and financial endurance that the current balance sheet constrains.
Key trigger: Monitor Xerox's revenue mix: IT services and Elem revenue collectively reaching 25%+ of total by FY2028 is the minimum scale to partially offset print decline. Immediate signal: whether free cash flow remains positive through FY2026. If it turns negative, transformation investment capacity is structurally impaired.

  • Lumentum is a pure-play AI infrastructure beneficiary with one of the highest revenue growth rates in the ADS universe. Q3 FY2026 revenue of $808M (+90% YoY) with $400M+ OCS backlog and an incremental multi-hundred-million-dollar CPO order confirms hyperscaler AI infrastructure investment is creating extraordinary demand for Lumentum's photonic components — optical circuit switches and co-packaged optics. The physics of AI cluster scale determines Lumentum's demand trajectory: GPU-to-GPU bandwidth requirements scale with the square of GPU cluster size.
  • Lumentum's optical circuit switch (OCS) is the enabling technology for next-generation AI cluster architectures. Traditional electrical switches cannot provide the bandwidth density and reconfigurability required for AI training workloads that dynamically redistribute compute across GPU pools. OCS enables AI cluster architects to reconfigure fabric topology in microseconds. Google, Microsoft, and Amazon have all publicly committed to OCS deployment for AI cluster infrastructure — creating the multi-quarter backlog providing Lumentum revenue visibility through FY2027.
  • Co-packaged optics (CPO) is Lumentum's next growth catalyst. Traditional pluggable optical transceivers consume 20–30% of the power of an AI server's networking bandwidth — prohibitive at 100,000+ GPU cluster scales. CPO reduces power consumption 70–80%. NVIDIA's next-generation AI server architecture is designed around CPO integration and Lumentum's incremental multi-hundred-million CPO order represents production-scale commitment. An ADS of 16 — among the lowest in the ADS universe — reflects essentially zero AI displacement risk and maximum AI infrastructure demand exposure.
Key trigger: Lumentum's OCS backlog conversion rate is the production execution indicator. Monitor whether gross margins expand toward 50%+ as OCS volume scales. The CPO order delivery timeline (H1 2027) is the next major revenue catalyst: successful on-time delivery validates Lumentum's CPO manufacturing capability and positions it for follow-on CPO orders from additional hyperscalers.
